## What to do
When you notice a strong feeling, take a few minutes and jot down five things:
1. **Situation** — where you were and what was happening.
2. **Feeling** — name it in a word, and rate it 0–100%.
3. **Thought** — what went through your mind, almost word for word.
4. **A second look** — one fact that fits the thought, and one fact that doesn't.
5. **Fairer thought** — a more balanced way to see it, then re-rate the feeling.

You can use your phone notes or paper — whatever you'll actually use.

## Why it helps
Strong feelings often ride on a fast, unchecked thought. Writing it down slows the
moment so you can see the thought instead of just believing it — the same steps we
do together, now in your own hands.

## How the agent introduces it
"If you're up for it, here's a small experiment for this week. When a strong feeling
shows up, you jot down the situation, the feeling, the thought behind it, and then
one fact for and one against it. Just noticing is the whole point — would you like to
try that?"

## Follow-up next session
Ask what they noticed, not whether they "did it right": "What did you spot when you
slowed a moment down? Was anything surprising?" If they didn't do it, treat that as
useful information — what got in the way? — and make it smaller.

## Make it easier if needed
On a hard week, just do steps 1–3 (situation, feeling, thought). Catching the thought
at all is the win.
