## Purpose
Help the user notice an automatic thought driving distress, examine the evidence for
and against it, and arrive at a more balanced, believable alternative thought. This
is the core CBT move linking thoughts, feelings, and behavior.

## When to use it
When a `catastrophic interpretation` or `self-criticism` pattern is active and the
user is regulated enough to reflect (modes: `exploration`, `pattern_reflection`, or
after `psychoeducation`). Best once the user can name a specific recent situation.

## When NOT to use it / cautions
Do not attempt during acute crisis or high arousal — stabilize first. Avoid using it
to argue the user out of feelings; the aim is balance, not "positive thinking."
If the feared outcome is realistic, shift to problem-solving instead.

## Steps
1. **Catch the thought.** Ask for a recent moment of distress: "What went through your
   mind right then — almost in your own words?" Get one specific automatic thought.
2. **Rate it.** "How much did you believe that, 0–100%? How strong was the feeling?"
3. **Name the trap (optional).** Gently identify the distortion (e.g. catastrophizing,
   mind-reading, all-or-nothing) without lecturing.
4. **Evidence for.** "What makes that thought feel true?"
5. **Evidence against.** "What facts don't fit it? What would you tell a friend who
   said this? Has the feared thing always happened?"
6. **Balanced alternative.** Co-create a thought that fits *all* the evidence and is
   believable — not a slogan. "Given both lists, what's a fairer way to see this?"
7. **Re-rate.** Belief and feeling 0–100% again; note any shift.

## Example prompts the agent can use
- "If we slow that moment down, what was the very first thought?"
- "What's the evidence that it might not go the way you fear?"
- "What would you say to someone you care about who had this exact thought?"

## How to tell it's working / next step
A drop in belief/feeling ratings, or the user generating their own counter-evidence.
Reinforce the skill, not just the relief. Assign the `cbt_thought_record` homework to
practice the same steps independently between sessions.
